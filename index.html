Ningquan Gu

Abstract

Sound plays a vital role in daily life, especially when visual information is limited. However, incorporating sound into robotic manipulation remains challenging. To address this, we propose SonicAloha, a system that integrates airborne audio sensing into the Aloha platform to enable multimodal manipulation through End2End imitation learning. We collect synchronized audio segment, vision, and joint data at each timestep. Concatenated audio segments are paired with the corresponding visual and joint inputs for training. To encode the audio information, we introduce the Audio Encoding Module, which downsamples audio waveforms, converts them into long mel-spectrograms, and encodes them using a fine-tuned CNN14 network to extract audio features. To fuse encoded audio and vision features, we propose the Transformer-based Bidirectional Cross-Attention Module, which combines two parallel cross-attention branches that enable dynamic mutual attention between modalities, and feeds the fused features into the Action Chunking with Transformers policy to generate predicted actions. Experimentally, we introduce three long-horizon, audio-visual bimanual tasks: alarm shutting, box locking, and stapler checking. Compared to vision-only baseline and existing audio-visual method, ours achieves superior performance. Moreover, it demonstrates robustness to closed-loop control, audio-visual noise, and zero-shot generalization.
